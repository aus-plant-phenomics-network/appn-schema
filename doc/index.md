## About the APPN Schema and FAIR Data

The APPN schema provides a structured way to describe plant‑phenotyping experiments, environments, sensors, biological materials, and measurements across the Australian Plant Phenomics Network. By standardising how experiments, variables, samples, observations, and platforms are described, the schema helps ensure that phenomics data are:
  
**Findable** — Common definitions and metadata structures make APPN datasets discoverable across multiple institutions and facilities.  
**Accessible** — APPN will provide open access to its phenotyping data and the schma supports consistent metadata standards across the national network.  
**Interoperable** — Standardised descriptions allow data from different platforms, sensors, and environments to work together across APPN’s diverse research nodes.  
**Reusable** — Rich contextual metadata about growth conditions, imaging systems, sensors, and methods helps researchers reliably interpret and reuse data for future studies.   
  
Together, the schema and its FAIR-aligned data descriptions enable robust, repeatable, and comparable plant‑science research across Australia’s coordinated phenomics infrastructure.
